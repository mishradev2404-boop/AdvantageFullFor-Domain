Lead Gen Forms vs. Landing Pages

The biggest mistake B2B advertisers make on LinkedIn is sending hard-earned clicks to external landing pages that fail to convert. LinkedIn's native Lead Gen Forms (LGFs) drastically outperform traditional landing pages for one simple reason: reduced friction. Member data is pre-filled, there is zero page load drop-off, and they are inherently mobile-optimised.

Criteria Native Lead Gen Forms External Landing Page
Friction Low (Pre-filled data) High (Manual entry)
Mobile UX Flawless (Native app) Variable (Depends on site)
Avg Completion Rate 10% - 20%+ 2% - 5%
Cost Per Lead (CPL) Typically Lower Typically Higher
Setup Time Minutes Days/Weeks

When to still use a Landing Page

While native forms are generally superior, landing pages have their place. Use them for complex offerings that require significant explanatory copy, long-form qualification where you need to filter out low-intent prospects aggressively, or when building specific pixel-based retargeting audiences based on deeper site engagement is critical to your funnel.

Choosing the Right Campaign Objective

LinkedIn's algorithm is literal. It gives you exactly what you ask for. Choosing between the Lead Generation and Website Conversions objective dictates not just the ad format, but who in your audience actually sees the ad based on their historical behavior.

Lead Generation

  • Uses native Lead Gen Forms (LGFs).
  • Algorithm optimises for users likely to open and submit forms.
  • Best for: Gated content, webinars, newsletters, direct contact requests.

Website Conversions

  • Drives traffic to external landing pages.
  • Requires LinkedIn Insight Tag to track success.
  • Best for: High-ticket purchases, free trials, complex software demos.

Technical Requirement

Regardless of which objective you choose, install the LinkedIn Insight Tag on your website immediately. Even if you use native forms, you'll want the tag for website retargeting audiences and tracking post-lead behavior.

Targeting Strategy for Lead Quality

Targeting is a delicate balance between volume and quality. Narrow audiences (under 50k) often deliver lower CPLs initially but fatigue quickly. Broad audiences maintain volume but risk bringing in unqualified leads. We solve this using an Audience Tiering strategy.

1

Tier 1: The Exact ICP

Highly specific targeting using exact Job Titles (e.g., "VP of Marketing") + specific Company Sizes or Target Account Lists. Expect higher CPL but maximum lead-to-MQL conversion.

2

Tier 2: Adjacent Roles & Functions

Broader approach using Job Functions (e.g., "Marketing") + Seniority (e.g., "Director, VP, CXO"). Helps scale volume when Tier 1 exhausts, catching influencers in the buying committee.

3

Tier 3: Skills & Groups (Broad)

Targeting based on Member Skills or LinkedIn Groups joined, paired with seniority filters. High volume, lowest CPL, but requires strict CRM qualification filtering.

Watch Out: Audience Expansion

Always disable LinkedIn's "Enable Audience Expansion" checkbox for lead generation campaigns. It gives the algorithm permission to target outside your defined parameters, often leading to wasted spend on irrelevant prospects.

The Ultimate Hack

Use Matched Audiences for your highest-intent targeting. Uploading custom contact lists or retargeting website visitors (who haven't converted yet) consistently yields the lowest CPL and highest closing rates.

Bid Strategy & Budget Optimisation

LinkedIn's auction for Lead Gen campaigns optimises for predicted conversion probability. Should you use Manual CPC or Automated bidding? The answer dictates your campaign's ability to scale. Here is our proven optimisation cadence.

W1

The Learning Phase

Launch with Automated Bidding (Max Delivery) to gather data quickly. Set daily budgets.

Golden Rule: Never pause or edit a campaign in the first 7 days. Let the algorithm learn.
W2

Pruning & Review

Review initial CPL and lead quality. Pause underperforming creatives. If CPL is too high, test switching to Target Cost bidding slightly below your desired CPL to force efficiency.

W3+

Scaling Winners

Scale daily budgets on winning campaigns by 15-20% every few days. Introduce new creative variations to combat ad fatigue while keeping the core targeting intact.

Budget Distribution Architecture

Avoid putting all your budget into one campaign group. Allocate roughly 60% to Cold Prospecting (Tier 1 & 2), 30% to Warm Retargeting (site visitors, company page engagement), and keep 10% for Testing new formats or wild-card audiences.

Document Ads: The Most Underrated Lead Gen Format

Document Ads let users read a multi-page PDF — an infographic, report, or case study — natively inside the LinkedIn feed before deciding whether to submit their details. This "try before you buy" dynamic makes them one of the highest-converting formats available for gated content.

In-feed Preview

Members can swipe through multiple pages before committing to the form. By the time they submit, they've already consumed your content — meaning intent is far higher than a cold form click.

Pre-Qualified Leads

Someone who reads 6 of 8 slides before submitting is a fundamentally warmer lead than someone who clicked on a static image. Document Ad engagement signals create a natural self-selection filter.

Higher Dwell Time

LinkedIn's algorithm rewards ads that keep users on-platform. A document users spend 45 seconds reading generates strong engagement signals — which lowers your CPL via improved auction scores over time.

Best Content Types for Document Ads

Match your document format to your audience's intent level

01

Data-Rich Infographics

One key stat or insight per page. Works best when each slide can stand alone as a takeaway. Ideal for industry benchmarks, trend reports, or framework visualisations. Gate at slide 4–5, not before.

02

Case Studies (Problem → Solution → Result)

The three-act structure works perfectly across 6–10 slides. Open with the customer's pain, show the intervention, close with a quantified result. Gate the "results" page to drive submissions from readers who are already bought in.

03

Step-by-Step Playbooks

"How to" frameworks with actionable steps per page. High perceived value, easy to consume on mobile. Gate after step 3 of 7 to hook readers mid-process — they're motivated to complete it.

04

Benchmark Reports

Proprietary or aggregated survey data that readers can't find elsewhere. Scarcity of original research makes this consistently the highest-converting document type. Tease 2 findings free, gate the full dataset.

The Gating Sweet Spot

Where you place the gate inside your document dramatically affects both completion rate and lead quality. Here's how the trade-off works:

Gate at slide 1–2

High volume, low intent. The reader hasn't consumed any value yet — expect high drop-off rates and lower lead quality.

Gate at slide 3–5 ✓

The sweet spot. Enough free value to build trust, enough content remaining to motivate submission. Best CPL-to-quality ratio.

Gate at final slide

Lowest volume — most readers have already consumed the full document and feel less urgency to submit. Reserve for very high-value assets.

Pro Tip: Repurpose Existing Assets

You don't need to create document ads from scratch. Repurpose existing blog posts, slide decks, or sales enablement materials into a well-designed PDF. The format matters more than the length — clean typography, one idea per slide, and a consistent brand palette will consistently outperform dense, wordy documents.

Measuring & Improving Lead Quality

Generating leads is easy; generating revenue-generating leads is hard. The friction you add to your Lead Gen Form dictates quality. Fewer fields = more volume. More custom fields = higher quality.

The Tiered Field Approach

Instead of asking for everything upfront, tier your data collection based on the offer value:

  • Low Friction Email, First Name, Last Name (Autofills instantly) - Best for generic newsletters.
  • Med Friction + Job Title, Company Name - Best for standard ebooks/guides.
  • High Friction + Work Email (custom field), Phone Number - For demo requests & high-value reports.

Crucial Step: Always integrate LinkedIn native forms directly to your CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce) via native integrations or Zapier. Speed to lead is critical. Connect marketing data back to sales outcomes to close the feedback loop.

The Metrics That Matter

CPL
Cost Per Lead

Total spend divided by number of leads generated. Track over time.

Form Open Rate
Opens / Clicks

Measures intent. Are clicks accidental or genuinely interested?

Completion Rate
Submits / Opens

Target >15%. If lower, reduce form fields or check offer alignment.

Lead-to-MQL Rate
Marketing Qualified

Percentage of leads that actually fit your target persona criteria.

Lead-to-SQL Rate
Sales Qualified

The ultimate quality metric. Leads accepted by sales for follow-up.
